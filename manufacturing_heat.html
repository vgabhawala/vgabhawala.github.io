Manufacturing & Heat Transfer Projects

Project 1: Manufacturing – Stirling Engine

Project Timeline: January 2022 – May 2022

Designed and fabricated a fully functional Stirling engine to demonstrate principles of thermodynamics and mechanical motion. The project emphasized precision design using CAD, application of Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T), and hands-on manufacturing using a variety of machining methods.

Components

Flywheel: Precision-machined aluminum disk turned on a lathe, then chamfered and balanced to ensure smooth engine operation.

Crank Arm & Linkages: Brass components milled to spec, drilled and reamed for fits, and deburred for smooth mechanical articulation.

Power Piston & Cylinder: Turned from aluminum and steel with polished surfaces to minimize leakage and friction.

Base Plate & Brackets: Fabricated using shearing and press brake operations. Holes were CNC drilled for accurate alignment.

Project 2: Heat Exchanger

Project Timeline: August 2022 – December 2022

Fabricated, tested, and evaluated the performance of a counterflow heat exchanger. A custom 3D printed baffle was incorporated to enhance thermal transfer efficiency and turbulence within the shell side.

Components

PVC Shell: Bandsaw-cut and deburred. Precision-drilled inlet/outlet holes. Epoxy-sealed press-fit fittings ensured leak-free flow paths.

Internal Copper Coil: Helically bent using a tube bender. Even spacing and no kinks to maintain pressure and maximize surface area.

3D Printed Baffle: Designed in Fusion 360 with spiral cutouts, 3D printed with PLA, sanded to fit snugly inside the PVC pipe and induce turbulence.
