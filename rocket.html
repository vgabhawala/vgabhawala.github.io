Bottle Rocket – Dynamics Laboratory

Project Timeline: January 2022 – April 2022

Introduction

We compared theoretically computed rocket trajectories with actual launch data to evaluate the limits of MATLAB-based kinematics/dynamics models in real-world conditions.

Materials and Methods

1.5 L bottle, water, rubber plug, air pump, plastic fins, nose cone, altimeter. Bottles were partially filled, pressurized, and released; an altimeter in the nose recorded peak altitude. Measured geometry: nozzle Ø 18 mm, bottle Ø 80 mm, length 250 mm.

Theoretical Calculations

Key relations used for exit velocity, mass flow, drag, thrust, and motion integration:

v_exit = √( 2 (P₁ − P₂) / ρ ) ṁ = ρ · v_exit · A_nozzle F_D = ½ · ρ_air · C_D · A_ref · v² F_T = P · A_nozzle F_net = F_T − (m_bottle + m_water − ṁ·t) g − F_D a = F_net / (m_bottle + m_water) v_t = v_{t−Δt} + a Δt s_t = s_{t−Δt} + v_t Δt

Estimated CD varied between ~0.345–0.379 across flight phases (coast/descent). MATLAB stepped the dynamics until apogee.

Trial 1 (Theory)

40 psi, 150 mL → Max thrust ≈ 70.2 N, peak altitude ≈ 32 m, thrust duration ≈ 0.505 s.

Trial 2 (Theory)

32 psi, 200 mL → Max thrust ≈ 56.14 N, peak altitude ≈ 31.5 m, thrust duration ≈ 0.619 s.

Trial 3 (Theory)

45 psi, 250 mL → Max thrust ≈ 78.95 N, peak altitude ≈ 37.9 m, thrust duration ≈ 0.619 s.

Experimental Results

Trial 1 (40 psi, 150 mL)

Peak altitude ≈ 29.57 m; thrust active ≈ 0.51 s. (Reported max thrust entry ≈ 70.2 N.)

Trial 2 (32 psi, 200 mL)

Peak altitude ≈ 30.48 m; thrust active ≈ 0.72 s. (Reported max thrust entry ≈ 70.2 N.)

Trial 3 (45 psi, 250 mL)

Peak altitude ≈ 35.5 m; thrust active ≈ 0.72 s. (Reported max thrust entry ≈ 78.95 N.)

Discussion

Apogees were within ~10% of theory across all trials; flight times (~6 s total) broadly agreed. Differences stem from uncertain CD, changing cross-section during descent, wind, and the model’s assumption of constant internal pressure (real pressure decays as water volume changes), which lengthened thrust in experiments.

Appendix

Early attempts estimated mass flow rate (ṁ) from steep trajectory segments and time-to-empty, yielding an unrealistically low value (~0.968 kg/s). Recomputing via Bernoulli gave ṁ ≈ 5.24 kg/s and much closer agreement to flight data. CD values were selected from literature and tuned per phase to reflect observed behavior.
