Electronics & Instrumentation – Peak & Trough Detector

Project Timeline: Fall 2023

In this project, we designed and fabricated a circuit that tracks the high and low voltage extremes from a temperature sensor. Such a device has applications in vaccine storage, wine and food preservation, and any setting where monitoring maximum and minimum temperatures is critical.

Concept & Design

A potentiometer simulated the varying temperature input. Using op-amps, diodes, and capacitors, the circuit stored peak (maximum) and trough (minimum) voltages. Separate reset buttons allowed users to clear each stored value. TinkerCAD was used to test different capacitor values before physical implementation.

Calculations

To size the capacitor for stable voltage storage, we used the standard RC time constant:

τ = R × C

Where:

  • τ = time constant (s)
  • R = resistance in the charging path (Ω)
  • C = capacitance (F)

To ensure the stored voltage held long enough for observation, we set:

τ ≥ 5 × thold

Initial estimates used C = 1 µF, but experimental testing showed that much higher values (≈1.5 mF) were required to prevent discharge through leakage and diode forward currents.

Performance

Oscilloscope testing confirmed accurate measurement of both peak and trough voltages. Reset buttons correctly returned stored voltages to baseline values. Higher capacitance than initially estimated was required (1 µF → 1.5 mF) to stabilize voltage storage.

Lessons Learned

  • TinkerCAD simulations accelerated troubleshooting and design validation.
  • Hardware issues mostly arose from diode failures, requiring replacements.
  • Capacitor choice was critical; higher capacitance improved stability.
  • Best performance was achieved when capacitors were referenced to ±5V rather than ground.
