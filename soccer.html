Desktop Soccer Game

Project Timeline: March 2024 – May 2024

This desktop-scale soccer game combines mechatronics and mechanical design: a user-controlled kicker shoots against a moving goalkeeper on a linear actuator. The build emphasized quick prototyping, clean integration, and playful interaction.

Components

  • Arduino Uno
  • HiLetgo L298N Motor Driver
  • NEMA11 Stepper Linear Rail Actuator
  • Gikfun 2" 4Ω 3W Speaker
  • 3D-Printed Flexible Kicker Mechanism
  • Laser-Cut Base & Structure
  • LED Display
  • Goal Frame with printed goalkeeper
  • Breadboard + jumper wires, resistors, capacitors
  • Power: 5V (logic), 12V (actuator)

Ideation

To keep the footprint minimal and setup fast, the desk became the foundation instead of a bulky base. Early cardboard trials validated joystick ergonomics and goal placement before committing to prints and laser-cut parts.

Design Process

The kicker and ball holder were 3D-printed. TPU was selected for flexibility and durability; reduced infill increased springiness and shot power. The goal was designed to align with the actuator-driven goalkeeper. Acrylic field boundaries enabled quick assembly and a clean look.

Video 1. Prototype test.

Electronics Added

A two-player scoreboard uses an Arduino-driven LCD. A printed enclosure houses mechanical buttons to increment/reset scores. The modular casing simplifies service and future features (automatic scoring, sound feedback).

Goalkeeper Integration

The linear actuator behind the goal sweeps the goalkeeper at random intervals via motor driver control, raising difficulty and adding unpredictability. Motion is synchronized through the Arduino for responsive gameplay.
